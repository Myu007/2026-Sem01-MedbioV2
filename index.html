Pediatric Gastroenterology

Chronic Gastroduodenitis

A systematic guide to the etiology, pathogenesis, classification, and treatment of CGD in children.

Definition

[span_0](start_span)Chronic gastroduodenitis (CGD) is a chronic, recidivating, inflammatory-dystrophic defeat of the mucous membrane of the stomach and duodenum[span_0](end_span). It is disposed to progressing.

85-90% Combined Disease [span_1](start_span)

Stomach + Duodenum involved[span_1](end_span)

10-15% Isolated Disease [span_2](start_span)

Stomach OR Duodenum only[span_2](end_span)

Anatomical Focus

Antrum (Stomach) & Bulb (Duodenum)

[attachment_0](attachment)

Etiology & Causes

Helicobacter Pylori (Hp)

85% of Cases

[span_3](start_span)A spiral gram-negative stick with affinity to the antral epithelium[span_3](end_span).

    [span_4](start_span)
  • Produces Urease (Splits urea -> Ammonia -> Alkaline pH) [cite: 21-22].
  • Stimulates G-cells -> Increases Gastrin -> Increases Acid Secretion.
[cite_start]

Alimentary Factors[span_4](end_span)

Irregular food, dry food, spicy/fatty food, lack of protein/vitamins, food additives.

[span_5](start_span)

Meds & Habits[span_5](end_span)

NSAIDs (non-steroid anti-inflammatory), antibiotics, steroids, smoking.

Autoimmune (Type A)

[span_6](start_span)

Formation of antibodies to facing (parietal) cells[span_6](end_span).

  • Rare in children (1-3%).
    • [span_7](start_span)
  • Leads to fast atrophy without active inflammation[span_7](end_span).

Reactive (Type C)

Exogenic-Endogenic group (10-12%).

    [span_8](start_span)
  • Reflux: Duodenal-Gastric Reflux (DGR) due to increased pressure[span_8](end_span).
  • Chemical: Irritation by medicines.

Clinical Syndromes

Ulcer-Shaped

Hp-Associated
  • Pain Pattern [span_9](start_span)Night pain, empty stomach, or 1.5-2 hrs after food[span_9](end_span).
  • Food Relation [span_10](start_span)Pain disappears after eating[span_10](end_span).
  • Endoscopy Antroduodenitis (Antrum + Bulb). [span_11](start_span)Edema & Hyperemia [cite: 50-51].

Gastritis-Shaped

Autoimmune / Long Hp
  • Pain Pattern [cite_start]Early pain (immediately or soon after meal)[span_11](end_span).
  • Dyspepsia [span_12](start_span)Heaviness, fullness, nausea, low appetite[span_12](end_span).
  • Endoscopy Diffuse damage or Stomach Body. [span_13](start_span)Atrophy possible [cite: 58-59].

Treatment Protocols

Diet should be mechanically, chemically, and thermally sparing. [cite_start]Frequency: 4-5 times/day[span_13](end_span).

Table No. 1 (High Secretion)

Milk, boiled mash, dairy. [span_14](start_span)
Avoid: Rich broths, sour juices[span_14](end_span).

Table No. 2 (Low Secretion)

[span_15](start_span)

Rich broths, vegetable salads, sour juices allowed[span_15](end_span).

[span_16](start_span)

Threefold Scheme[span_16](end_span)

Standard for Normal Secretion (2 Weeks)

  • De-Nol (Bismuth): 120mg 3-4x/day.
  • Antibiotic: Amoxicillin (50mg/kg) OR Clarithromycin.
  • Metronidazole: 15mg/kg.
[span_17](start_span)

Double Scheme[span_17](end_span)

For Increased Secretion (2 Weeks)

  • PPI (Omeprazole): 1-2mg/kg once at night.
  • Antibiotic: Amoxicillin OR Clarithromycin.
    [span_18](start_span)
  • Antacids: Almagel, Maalox (1hr after meal)[span_18](end_span).
    • [span_19](start_span)
  • Antisecretory: Ranitidine (H2), Omeprazole (PPI) [cite: 78-79].
    • [cite_start]
  • Prokinetics: Motilium, Cerucal (For Reflux)[span_19](end_span).
    • [span_20](start_span)
  • Reparative: Vitamins, Carnitine, Mumie, Propolis (For Atrophy) [cite: 96-100].
